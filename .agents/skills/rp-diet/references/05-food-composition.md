# Ch 5: Food Composition
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
~5% of body comp/performance results. Much larger impact on long-term health. Get ~75% from quality sources; rest can be flexible.

---

## PROTEIN COMPOSITION

### Digestibility (PDCAAS Score, 0-1)
| Source | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Whey, casein, egg | 1.00 |
| Soy isolate, mycoprotein | 0.99 |
| Beef | 0.92 |
| Chickpeas | 0.78 |
| Black beans | 0.75 |
| Peanuts | 0.52 |
| Rice | 0.50 |
| Whole wheat | 0.42 |
| Wheat gluten | 0.25 |

- Animal sources: high digestibility, complete amino acids
- Plant sources: lower digestibility (cellulose barrier), often incomplete
- Processed plant protein can be better than whole (processing breaks cellulose)

### Complete vs Complementary
- **Complete**: Contains all essential amino acids (all animal sources)
- **Complementary**: Multiple incomplete sources combined (beans + rice)

## CARBOHYDRATE COMPOSITION

### Glycemic Index (0-100)
How fast carbs hit bloodstream. Higher = faster spike.

| Source | GI |
|--------|-----|
| Dextrose powder | 100 |
| Gatorade | 90 |
| White potato | 85 |
| White rice | 65 |
| Sweet potato | 60 |
| Oatmeal | 55 |
| Whole-grain bread | 50 |
| Brown rice | 50 |
| Fruits (apple, orange) | 40 |
| Kidney beans | 35 |
| Skim milk | 30 |

**Mixed meals lower GI**: Fat, protein, fiber slow digestion.

### Timing Application
| Window | GI Preference |
|--------|---------------|
| Pre-workout (close) | High GI |
| Pre-workout (far) | Low GI |
| Intra-workout | High GI (liquid) |
| Post-workout (early) | High GI |
| Post-workout (late) | Lower GI OK |
| Long meal intervals | Low GI |

### Micronutrient Density
- Veggies, fruits, whole grains = nutrient-rich
- Dextrose, white bread = nutrient-poor
- Vary sources for full vitamin/mineral spectrum

## FAT COMPOSITION

### Fat Types (% of daily fat intake)
| Type | Recommended | Sources |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Monounsaturated | 45-60% | Olive oil, avocado, nuts |
| Polyunsaturated | 35-50% | Fish, walnuts, seeds, canola |
| Saturated | 5-20% | Dairy, eggs, meat, coconut |
| Trans | <1% | Processed foods (avoid) |

### Key Points
- **Monounsaturated**: Best for health + body comp; prioritize
- **Omega-3s**: Often under-eaten; supplement if needed
- **Saturated**: Moderate; may support hormones but health risk if excessive
- **Trans**: Avoid; linked to cardiovascular disease + worse body comp

## MICRONUTRIENTS

### Vitamins
- Water-soluble: C, B-complex (fruits, veggies, grains)
- Fat-soluble: A, D, E, K (need dietary fat to absorb)
- B12 only in animal products; vegans must supplement

### Minerals & Electrolytes
- **Sodium/Chloride**: Easy from salt; replace after sweating
- **Potassium**: Potatoes, bananas
- **Magnesium**: Spinach, beans, dairy
- **Calcium**: Dairy, leafy greens
- **Iron**: Meat (heme, well-absorbed) > plants (non-heme, poorly absorbed)

Iron deficiency common in vegans, vegetarians, menstruating women. Supplement if needed.

### Fiber
- Not essential but supports: satiety, digestion, blood sugar stability
- Helpful on fat-loss diets

### Phytochemicals
- Plant compounds with potential health benefits
- Get via colorful fruits/veggies variety

---

## PRACTICAL APPLICATION
- ~75% quality sources → full benefit
- ~25% flexible (junk food OK if macros/calories met)
- As macros get more restricted (end of cut), food composition matters more
- Consider multivitamin during aggressive cuts
