# Ch 8: Hunger Management
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Hunger management strategies improve comfort and adherence. Use them progressively as needed—not all at once from day 1.

---

## FAT LOSS: Hunger Reduction Strategies

### 1. Higher Volume Foods
Stomach stretch signals fullness. Choose foods that fill space with fewer calories.
- **Best**: Vegetables, fruits
- **Good**: Lean proteins, whole grains
- **Avoid**: Dense calorie bombs (nuts, oils, fried foods)

### 2. Lower Palatability Foods
Tasty food → want more → harder to stop. Boring food → easier to stop.
| Palatability | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| Low (best for cut) | Vegetables, plain oatmeal, plain potatoes |
| Moderate | Whole grain bread, pasta, rice, beans |
| High | French fries, ice cream |
| Very High (avoid) | Chips, cookies, candy bars |

### 3. Increase Protein
Most satiating macro. Raise protein even above minimum if it helps adherence (trade-off: fewer carbs).

### 4. Bias Meal Timing
Move calories to when you're hungriest (often evening). Smaller morning meals, larger evening meals if that's your pattern.

### 5. Higher Fiber Foods
- Veggies, fruits, whole grains
- Slows digestion = longer fullness

### 6. Reduce Liquid Calories
Easy to overconsume. Drop intra-workout shakes if needed; add those calories to whole food meals.

### 7. Drink Before Meals + Eat Slowly
- 8-24 oz calorie-free liquid before eating
- Stretch stomach before food arrives
- Eat slowly; fullness signals take time

### 8. Increase Caffeine
- Appetite suppressant
- Boosts energy and NEAT
- Use diet sodas, black coffee, sugar-free energy drinks

---

## MUSCLE GAIN: Hunger Promotion Strategies

### 1. Lower Volume Foods
Less stomach stretch = easier to eat more.
- White rice > oatmeal
- Pasta > vegetables
- Grains > fruits

### 2. Higher Palatability Foods
Tasty food → want more → easier to eat enough.
- Better seasonings, cooking methods
- Some junk food OK (especially late in phase)

### 3. Decrease Protein (to minimum)
- 0.8 g/lb sufficient on hypercaloric diet
- Frees calories for less filling options

### 4. Bias Meal Timing
Move food to hungrier times; eat more when appetite is higher.

### 5. Lower Fiber Foods
- White rice instead of brown
- Still get enough fiber from volume; don't need extra

### 6. Increase Liquid Calories
- Protein shakes, carb powders, whole milk
- Around workouts especially
- Less stomach fullness per calorie

### 7. Avoid Pre-Meal Liquids + Eat Faster
- Don't fill stomach with water before meals
- Eat without long pauses; finish before fullness kicks in

### 8. Decrease Caffeine
- Appetite suppressant; skip when trying to gain
- Save sensitivity for when you need it (fat loss)

---

## Practical Application
- **Start of diet**: Minimal hunger management needed
- **Mid-diet**: Add strategies as discomfort increases
- **Deep into diet**: Use most/all strategies for adherence
- Don't deploy everything day 1—save tools for when needed
