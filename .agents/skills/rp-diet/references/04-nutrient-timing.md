# Ch 4: Nutrient Timing
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
~10% of diet effectiveness. Small but significant—can determine podium placement in competitive sport.

## Six Components of Timing
1. **Meal number**: How many meals/day
2. **Meal spacing**: Hours between meals
3. **Meal size**: Calories per meal
4. **Meal macros**: Macro distribution per meal
5. **Meal composition**: Food types (digestion speed)
6. **Timing around activity**: Pre/intra/post workout meals

## Meal Frequency Guidelines
- **Recommended**: 4-8 meals/day, evenly spaced
- **Avoid extremes**: <3 meals (too large) or 10+ meals (never satisfied)
- Match meal size to intermeal interval

## Protein Timing

### Per-Meal Protein
- **Minimum**: 1/8 of daily protein per meal
- **Maximum**: 1/4 of daily protein per meal (excess not used for muscle)
- **Optimal**: Even distribution across 4-6 meals

### Why It Matters
- Body can only use ~1/4 daily protein per meal for muscle synthesis
- Long gaps without protein = muscle breakdown
- FSR (muscle building) stays elevated 1-3 days post-training; constant amino acids needed

### Example (200g daily protein)
- Per meal: 25-50g protein
- 4 meals × 50g or 6 meals × 33g

## Carb Timing

### Glycogen Synthesis Limits
- Max absorption: ~0.3-0.4 g/lb/hour
- 150 lb person: ~50g carbs/hour max utilization

### Timing Priority
Bias carbs toward activity:
1. **Pre-workout** (highest)
2. **Post-workout** (high)
3. **Intra-workout** (if >60 min)
4. **High-activity periods**
5. **Low-activity periods** (lowest)

## Fat Timing
- Bias **away** from training (slows digestion)
- Best for: longer intermeal intervals, bedtime
- Minimal fat in post-workout meal

## Activity Windows

### Pre-Training (30 min - 4 hrs before)
- Top off glycogen, stabilize blood glucose
- Closer to training → smaller meal, faster-digesting foods
- Further from training → larger meal OK

### Intra-Training (during workout)
- Relevant for >60 min sessions
- Fast-digesting carbs + small protein dose
- ~5-10% daily protein per hour if needed

### Post-Training (0-6 hrs after)
- Muscles highly sensitive to carbs
- Fat cells less sensitive = better partitioning
- Eat ASAP when comfortable
- Fast-digesting protein + carbs; minimal fat
- Glycogen resynthesis: 30-60g carbs/hour typical

### High-Activity Non-Training
- Increase carbs to match energy expenditure

### Low-Activity Periods
- Maintain steady protein
- Lower carb/fat needs

### Bedtime
- Slow-digesting protein (casein)
- Some fat OK (extends amino acid release)
- Eat close to bed, but not so close it disrupts sleep

## Key Numbers

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Meals/day | 4-8 |
| Hours between meals | 3-6 |
| Protein per meal | 1/8 to 1/4 daily total |
| Pre-workout meal | 30 min - 4 hrs before |
| Post-workout meal | ASAP to ~1 hr after |
| Carb max absorption | ~0.3 g/lb/hr |

## Practical Notes
- Don't be obsessive—ranges exist for flexibility
- Undereating one meal can be offset by overeating next
- Never sacrifice calories, macros, or sleep for perfect timing
