# Ch 1: The Diet Priorities
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Five principles determine diet effectiveness. Their impact is NOT equal—prioritize accordingly.

## Diet Priority Pyramid (by impact)

| Priority | Contribution | Focus |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| 1. Calorie Balance | ~50% | Calories in vs out |
| 2. Macronutrients | ~30% | Protein/carb/fat amounts |
| 3. Nutrient Timing | ~10% | Meal distribution |
| 4. Food Composition | ~5% | Food quality/sources |
| 5. Supplements/Hydration | ~5% | Pills, powders, water |

**Adherence** is prerequisite—best diet fails if not followed.

## Common Mistakes
- Obsessing over supplements while ignoring calories
- Perfect meal timing but inconsistent macros
- "Clean eating" without calorie awareness
- Effort wasted on low-priority factors

## Key Definitions

### Body Weight Concepts
- **Set Point**: Genetic predisposition for bodyweight
- **Settling Point**: Current preferred weight based on habits; can be pushed above set point with sustained surplus

### Diet Phases
- **Fat-Loss Phase**: Deficit to lose fat; minimize muscle loss
- **Muscle-Gain Phase**: Surplus to gain muscle; minimize fat gain
- **Post-Diet Maintenance**: Reset metabolism/psychology at new weight (weeks-months)
- **Long-Term Maintenance**: Sustain results indefinitely

### Training Types
- **High-Volume Hypertrophy**: 8-20+ sets/muscle/week, 6-30 reps; for gaining/retaining muscle
- **Low-Volume Strength**: 5-15 sets/muscle/week, 1-8 reps; for maintenance phases

### Other Terms
- **FSR**: Fractional Synthetic Rate—how fast protein becomes muscle
- **FBR**: Fractional Breakdown Rate—how fast muscle is lost
- **P-Ratio**: Partitioning ratio—muscle:fat ratio of weight gained/lost

### Training Experience
- **Beginner**: 0-3 years (gains come easy)
- **Intermediate**: 3-6 years
- **Advanced**: 6+ years (gains hard-won)
