# Ch 2: Calorie Balance
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Calorie balance = most powerful diet variable (~50% of results). Any deficit → weight loss. Any surplus → weight gain.

## Caloric States
- **Isocaloric/Eucaloric**: Intake = expenditure → weight stable
- **Hypocaloric**: Intake < expenditure → weight loss (body breaks down tissue)
- **Hypercaloric**: Intake > expenditure → weight gain (body stores excess)

## Calories and Performance
- Blood glucose + muscle glycogen = primary fuel for sport
- Undereating for 1+ week → degraded performance
- Fat breakdown too slow for high-intensity activity
- Recovery requires calories; deficit = slower/incomplete repair

## Calories and Body Composition
- Surplus → mostly fat gain (body primed by evolution to store)
- Muscle growth much slower than fat gain (muscle metabolically costly)
- Losing 15 lb fat in 3 months: realistic
- Gaining 15 lb muscle in 3 months: impossible

### Muscle Growth Requirements
1. Energy surplus + adequate protein (raw materials)
2. Progressive resistance training (stimulus)

## Recomposition (Simultaneous Muscle Gain + Fat Loss)
Generally inefficient. Fat loss needs deficit; muscle gain needs surplus. Exceptions:

| Case | Why It Works | Duration |
|------|--------------|----------|
| New to dieting | Novel stimulus | Several months |
| New to training | High demand burns fat | Several months |
| Pharmacological | Drugs override normal physiology | Until tolerance |

After novelty fades, separate phases needed for efficient progress.

## Calorie Demand Factors
- **Body size**: More cells = more energy needed
- **NEAT**: Non-exercise activity; often > formal exercise
- **Exercise/training**: Direct energy expenditure
- **Height/surface area**: More area = more heat loss = more calories
- **Stress**: Boosts metabolism (weight gain from stress = overeating or water)
- **Recovery demands**: Repair from training costs energy
- **Genetics**: Small metabolic efficiency differences compound over time
- **Body fat %**: Leaner = slightly higher maintenance
- **Drugs**: Stimulants (caffeine) slightly boost metabolic rate
- **Sex**: Men burn more (size, muscle mass, hormones)
