# Ch 15: Gut Health
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Gut microbiome affects metabolism, appetite, immunity. Healthy diet is the biggest factor—not supplements.

---

## MICROBIOME BASICS

- 100+ trillion microorganisms in gut
- ~10x more bacterial cells than human cells
- Profile influenced by: age, sex, drugs, exercise (~20%), diet (~20%)

### Effects on Body
- **Appetite**: Gut bacteria affect hunger hormones
- **Metabolism**: Certain bacteria linked to obesity/insulin resistance
- **Performance**: Correlations between fitness and bacterial profiles

---

## DIET EFFECTS ON MICROBIOME

### Carbohydrates
- Whole grains > refined grains (fiber feeds beneficial bacteria)
- High-fiber diet improves diversity
- **FODMAP note**: Some with IBS need low-FODMAP diet temporarily

### Fats
- High-fat diet (>40% calories) linked to:
  - Reduced diversity
  - Increased inflammation
  - Worse insulin sensitivity
- Omega-3s: neutral/positive effect
- Saturated fat: can degrade gut lining

**Recommendation**: Keep fat <40% of total calories

### Protein
- High protein linked to leanness profiles
- But also produces some concerning metabolites
- Red/processed meat: higher risk than poultry
- Varied protein sources likely best

### Artificial Sweeteners
- Mixed evidence; highly individual
- Sucralose: minimal effect at normal doses
- Sugar-sweetened beverages worse than diet versions

---

## PROBIOTICS

### What They Are
Live bacteria supplements or fermented foods (yogurt, kefir, kimchi, sauerkraut)

### Evidence
- May improve GI issues
- Strain-specific effects
- Cannot recolonize gut; only enriches populations
- Recent studies show risks/shortcomings

### Recommendations
- Not necessary for most people
- If using: look for USP label, ≥1 billion CFU
- High Bifidobacteria, low Lactobacilli
- Post-antibiotic: may delay natural recovery

---

## PREBIOTICS

Fibers that feed beneficial bacteria:
- Inulin, beta-glucan (wheat, barley, onions, asparagus)
- Resistant starch (potatoes, rice, legumes—cook/cool to increase)
- IMO syrup (low-GI sweetener with prebiotic effect)
- Polyphenols (fruits, berries)

**Easier approach**: Eat high-fiber whole foods

---

## PRACTICAL SUMMARY

**DO:**
- Eat primarily whole foods
- Include whole grains, vegetables, fruits
- Include fermented foods
- Include Omega-3 sources
- Keep fat <40% of calories

**LIMIT:**
- Red/processed meat
- Saturated fats (<10% calories)
- Refined sugars
- Some artificial sweeteners

**SKIP:**
- Collagen supplements for "gut healing" (no evidence)
- Glutamine supplements for gut (no evidence of benefit)
