# Ch 7: Diet Adherence
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Best diet fails if not followed. Adherence is prerequisite for all other principles to work.

## Choosing Diet Timing

### Avoid Diet Phases During:
- Weddings, vacations, social events
- High-stress periods
- Poor sleep periods
- Times with little meal control

### Best Times to Diet:
- Stable schedule
- Good sleep
- Low external stress
- Full control over meals

## Individual Tolerances

### Before Fat Loss, Honestly Assess:
- How much hunger can you handle?
- For how long?
- What deficit rate is sustainable for you?

### Before Muscle Gain, Honestly Assess:
- How much fat gain can you tolerate psychologically?
- Can you handle months of less definition?
- What surplus rate won't cause you to quit?

Trial and error helps find your personal limits.

## Six-Construct Model of Adherence

| Stage | Description | When It Matters |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Inspiration** | Initial spark; seeing a transformation, photo of yourself | Day 0; gets you started |
| **Motivation** | Defined goal; reason to continue | First weeks |
| **Intention** | Concrete plan; knowing what to eat tomorrow | Early phase |
| **Discipline** | Willpower when motivation fails | Mid-phase; hardest period |
| **Habit** | Automated behavior; dieting becomes easier | Weeks/months in |
| **Passion** | Love the process; adherence becomes effortless | Years in (optional) |

### Tips by Stage

**Inspiration**: Use positive inspiration (reward) over negative (shame)

**Motivation**: Keep goal visible—note on fridge, pants you want to fit, competition roster

**Intention**: Create detailed plan; meal prep on same day weekly

**Discipline**: Expect hard moments in advance; willpower is finite, use strategically

**Habit**: Same training days, same sleep/wake times, go-to healthy options ready

**Passion**: Choose goals for yourself (not others); connect with like-minded people

## Practical Recommendations

### Long-Term Thinking
- Focus on final goal, not temporary discomfort
- Trade-offs are temporary; donuts can return after fat loss

### Internal Locus of Control
- Don't blame external factors (work, airport)
- Ask: "What could I have done differently?"
- Each obstacle overcome = better prepared for next time

### Dedication
- Goal must be more important than temporary pleasures
- Strict adherence during diet → enjoyment returns in maintenance
