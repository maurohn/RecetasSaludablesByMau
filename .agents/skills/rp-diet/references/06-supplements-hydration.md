Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Combined ~5% of results. Drink to thirst for most situations. Most supplements don't work.

## HYDRATION

### General Rule
Drink when thirsty. For most people, this maintains adequate hydration for health AND performance.

### When to Be More Deliberate
- Hard training with lots of sweating
- Hot/humid environments
- Competition day

### Assessment Tools
1. **Urine color**: Light yellow = good; dark yellow = dehydrated; clear = overhydrated
2. **Bodyweight**: Track before/after training to assess sweat loss
3. **Guideline**: ~1.5 ml water per calorie eaten (3L for 2000 cal diet)

### Dehydration Levels (% bodyweight lost)
| Level | % Loss | Effects |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Mild | 1-2% | Thirst, mild fatigue, small performance drop |
| Moderate | 2-4% | Focus issues, bigger performance drop, overheating risk |
| Severe | 5-6% | Cramping, rapid pulse, possible heat exhaustion |
| Dangerous | 7%+ | Fainting, muscle spasms, possible heat stroke/death |

**Max safe water cut for competition**: 5% bodyweight

### Hydration Protocols

**Pre-training**: Arrive hydrated; monitor weight/urine color

**Intra-training**: ~5 oz per 20 min hard exertion; add electrolytes

**Post-training**: Drink 1.5x what you sweated out (~8 oz per 15 min until replenished)

### Hyponatremia Warning
Drinking lots of plain water (especially rapidly) without electrolytes can dilute blood sodium → dangerous. Always include electrolytes with heavy rehydration.

## SUPPLEMENTS

### Selection Criteria (must meet ALL)
1. Sufficient research data
2. 5+ year research history
3. Consensus of results
4. Positive real-world feedback

### Supplements That Actually Work (7 total)

| Supplement | Effect | Use Case |
|------------|--------|----------|
| **Caffeine** | Alertness, focus, pain tolerance, hunger suppression | Pre-workout, hard sessions |
| **Whey Protein** | Fast-digesting, highest quality protein | Intra/post workout |
| **Casein Protein** | Very slow-digesting (~7 hrs) | Bedtime, long gaps between meals |
| **Creatine** | ATP buffer; improves reps, strength, muscle retention | Daily; 3-5g/day |
| **Carb Formulas** | Fast-digesting energy | Intra/post workout |
| **Multivitamin** | Insurance for micronutrients | During cuts or poor food access |
| **Omega-3** | Essential fats often under-eaten | Daily if not eating fatty fish |

### Creatine Notes
- Monohydrate = best form (exotic versions less effective)
- May cause 1-3% bodyweight water gain (intramuscular; looks more muscular, not bloated)
- Water weight drops ~1 week after stopping
- Vegans/vegetarians benefit more (no dietary creatine from meat)

### Whey Protein Types
- **Concentrate**: Cheapest, effective, may cause GI issues in some
- **Isolate**: Purer, less GI issues, more expensive
- **Hydrolysate**: Purest, very expensive, for severe dairy sensitivity

### What Doesn't Work
Vast majority of supplements have no proven effect. If it's not on the list above, it probably doesn't work. Check Examine.com for research on specific supplements.
