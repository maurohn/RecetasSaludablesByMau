# Ch 3: Macronutrients
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
After calories, macros are ~30% of diet effectiveness. Protein > Carbs > Fats for body comp/performance.

## Calorie Content
- Protein: 4 cal/g
- Carbs: 4 cal/g
- Fat: 9 cal/g

## Caloric Constraint Hypothesis (CCH)
Macros must fit within calorie target. Raising one macro = lowering another. Can't optimize one in isolation.

---

## PROTEIN

### Why It Matters
- Literally builds muscle (amino acids = building blocks)
- Anti-catabolic (prevents muscle breakdown)
- Supports enzymes, connective tissue, hemoglobin

### Protein Recommendations (g/lb bodyweight/day)

| Context | Minimum | Optimal | Maximum |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| General Health | 0.3 | 0.5-0.8 | — |
| Endurance Sports | 0.5 | 0.7 | 1.0 |
| Team Sports | 0.6 | 0.8 | 1.5 |
| Strength/Power | 0.7 | 1.0 | 2.0 |
| Fat Loss (hypocaloric) | 0.8 | 1.0 | 1.5 |
| Muscle Gain (hypercaloric) | 0.7 | 1.0 | 1.5 |

**Key insight**: ~1g/lb/day works for most situations. Higher doesn't add muscle benefit but may help satiety.

---

## CARBOHYDRATES

### Why They Matter
- Primary fuel for high-intensity activity
- Powers nervous system (brain prefers glucose)
- Anti-catabolic via insulin + glycogen pathways
- Depleted glycogen = muscle loss risk

### Carb Types
- Monosaccharides: glucose, fructose, galactose
- Disaccharides: sucrose, lactose, maltose
- Polysaccharides: starch, glycogen, fiber

"Simple" vs "complex" labels are misleading—digestion speed varies independently of molecular size.

### Carb Recommendations (g/lb bodyweight/day)

| Context | Minimum | Optimal | Maximum |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| General Health | 0.3-0.5 | — | CCH limit |
| Endurance Sports | 1.5 | 2.0-3.0 | 5.0+ (race day) |
| Team Sports | 1.5 | 1.5-3.0 | — |
| Strength/Power | 1.0 | 1.5 | 2.5 |
| Fat Loss | 1.0 | CCH max | — |
| Muscle Gain | 1.0 | CCH max | — |

**Key insight**: Max out carbs within CCH constraints for both fat loss (muscle retention) and muscle gain (anabolism).

---

## FATS

### Why They Matter
- Essential fatty acids (Omega-3, Omega-6) required
- Hormone production (testosterone, estrogen)
- Vitamin absorption
- NOT a good performance fuel

### Fat Recommendations (g/lb bodyweight/day)

| Context | Minimum | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| All contexts | 0.3 | Health floor for hormones + essentials |
| Endurance | ~0.3 | Carbs take priority |
| Fat Loss | 0.3 | Cut fat first to preserve carbs |
| Muscle Gain | 0.3+ | Can go higher for palatability/comfort |

**Key insight**: Keep fats at minimum (~0.3g/lb) when carbs matter. Raise fats only when calories allow after protein + carb needs met.

---

## Macro Hierarchy
1. **Protein** > Carbs (literally builds muscle)
2. **Carbs** > Fat (better fuel, anti-catabolic)

### Exception: Endurance Sports
During heavy training/racing, carbs may temporarily outrank protein for acute performance.
