# Ch 10: Designing Your Diet
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Step-by-step process: Calories → Macros → Meal timing → Food composition

---

## STEP 1: CALCULATE CALORIES

### Activity Classification

| Day Type | Duration | Working Sets | Sweat | Calories Burned |
|----------|----------|--------------|-------|-----------------|
| Non-training | 0 | 0 | — | — |
| Light | <1 hr | <10 sets | Light | ~300 |
| Moderate | 1-2 hr | 10-25 sets | Half wet | ~500 |
| Hard | >2 hr | >25 sets | Soaked | ~1000 |

### Starting Calorie Estimates (per lb bodyweight)
| Day Type | Approx Calories |
|----------|-----------------|
| Non-training | ~12 cal/lb |
| Light | ~13-14 cal/lb |
| Moderate | ~14-15 cal/lb |
| Hard | ~16 cal/lb |

*These are rough starting points; adjust based on weight changes.*

---

## STEP 2: CALCULATE MACROS

### Order of Operations
1. **Protein first** (most important)
2. **Carbs second** (performance)
3. **Fat fills remainder** (CCH)

### Protein
**~1 g/lb bodyweight/day** (constant across all day types)

### Carbs by Day Type
| Day Type | Minimum | Recommended Start |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| Non-training | 0.3 g/lb | 0.5 g/lb |
| Light | 0.5 g/lb | 1.0 g/lb |
| Moderate | 1.0 g/lb | 1.5 g/lb |
| Hard | 1.5 g/lb | 2.0 g/lb |

### Fat
**Minimum**: 0.3 g/lb/day (health floor)
**Calculation**: (Total calories - protein calories - carb calories) ÷ 9

### Trade-offs
- Need more performance? Raise carbs, lower fats (stay above 0.3 g/lb fat)
- Prefer higher fat? Lower carbs (stay above carb minimums)

---

## STEP 3: DETERMINE MEAL TIMING

### Process
1. **Choose meal number**: 4-6 meals/day optimal
2. **Assign first meal**: Within 1 hr of waking
3. **Assign bedtime meal**: Within 1 hr of sleep
4. **Schedule pre-workout**: 30 min - 4 hrs before training
5. **Schedule post-workout**: Within 1 hr after training
6. **Fill remaining meals**: Evenly spaced (3-5 hr intervals)

### Protein Distribution
- Divide evenly across meals
- Example: 155g protein ÷ 5 meals = ~30g/meal
- Slightly more before longer intervals (bedtime)

### Carb Distribution (% of daily carbs by meal)
| Meal Window | Fat Loss | Muscle Gain |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Pre-workout | 25% | 20% |
| Intra-workout | 0-10% | 10% |
| Post-workout | 35% | 30% |
| Other meals | 30-40% | 40% |

### Fat Distribution
- Bias **away** from workout window
- More fat in: morning, bedtime, non-training day meals
- Less fat in: pre/post workout meals

---

## STEP 4: INTRA-WORKOUT NUTRITION

### When to Use
- Sessions >60 min
- Hard training days
- Multiple sessions/day

### Contents
- Fast-digesting carbs (sports drink, dextrose)
- Small protein dose (~10-20g whey)
- Minimal fat/fiber

---

## STEP 5: ADJUSTING FOR GOALS

### Fat Loss
- Subtract 300-500 cal/day from maintenance
- Or: ~15-25% calorie reduction
- Cut fats first, then carbs (preserve protein)

### Muscle Gain
- Add 200-400 cal/day to maintenance
- Or: ~10-15% calorie increase
- Add carbs first, then fats if needed

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES
- Start with maintenance, then adjust for goal
- Design once, then track and refine
- Err conservative on calorie estimates
- When uncertain: less food for fat loss, more food for muscle gain
