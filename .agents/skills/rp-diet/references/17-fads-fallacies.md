# Ch 17: Fads and Fallacies
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## Core Principle
Most diet fads ignore basic principles (calories, macros). Apply critical thinking; check claims against fundamentals.

---

## DEBUNKED TRENDS

### Butter/Fat in Coffee
- No magic benefit; just 500 calories of saturated fat
- Claim: reduces appetite. Reality: any 500 cal meal does
- Claim: avoids carb/cortisol issues. Reality: carbs reduce cortisol

### Intermittent Fasting
- No adherence advantage in research (some show worse adherence)
- Autophagy claim: any deficit increases autophagy; no special fasting benefit
- GH claim: elevated GH without nutrients = no anabolic effect
- Trade-off: worse muscle retention vs regular meals

### Carb Backloading
- Claims based on faulty hormone logic
- Insulin sensitivity is actually higher in morning
- Avoiding carbs = catabolism risk
- No evidence of benefit vs normal eating

### Cleanses/Detoxes
- Liver already processes toxins continuously
- No herbs "remove toxins"
- Often protein-deficient → muscle loss
- Benefits = just eating healthy + calorie reduction

### Alkaline/Acidic Diets
- Body regulates pH extremely tightly (7.35-7.45)
- Food pH has zero effect on blood/cellular pH
- Stomach acid neutralizes alkaline foods immediately
- "Essentially a scam"

## COMMON MYTHS

### "Inflammation Is Bad"
- Acute inflammation: necessary for recovery/adaptation
- Chronic inflammation: problematic
- Best intervention: weight loss (not special foods)

### "Digestive Problems Cause Weight Gain"
- Logic is backwards: poor digestion = fewer absorbed calories
- People with celiac/Crohn's are often underweight
- Digestive enzymes won't help weight loss

### "Hormones Cause Weight Gain"
- Deficit still causes weight loss regardless of hormones
- Herbal "hormone fixes" don't meaningfully alter hormones
- Over-dieting shifts hormones; solution = diet break

### "Eating at Night Makes You Fat"
- Research is equivocal; no consistent effect
- Calorie total matters far more than timing
- Spreading meals throughout day > condensed eating

### "Breakfast Is Most Important Meal"
- No evidence first meal is special
- Protein after overnight fast is good
- Doesn't need to be elaborate

## PERSISTENT MYTHS

### "Natural Is Better"
- Natural ≠ healthier or safer
- Cyanide is natural; whey protein is processed
- Assess each food on its merits

### "Processed Food Is Bad"
- Processing removes some nutrients (white flour)
- Processing can improve food (whey, vegan protein)
- Problem: processed food often replaces whole food

### "GMOs Are Dangerous"
- 30+ years of testing; 20+ years of use
- Zero confirmed cases of harm
- Scientific consensus: no more dangerous than conventional

### "Organic Is Healthier"
- No significant nutrient differences in research
- "Organic" still allows synthetic substances
- Only verified difference: price

### "Hormones in Meat"
- Residue levels are physiologically irrelevant
- Not orally bioavailable (liver breaks down)
- Eggs have 200x more estrogen than treated meat

### "Antibiotics in Meat"
- Animals must clear antibiotics before slaughter
- No trace quantities in store-bought meat
- Concern: global antibiotic resistance (different issue)

### "Chemicals Are Bad"
- All food is chemicals
- Natural chemicals can be harmful (cyanide in fruit pits)
- Most dietary carcinogens are natural plant compounds
- Assess evidence, not chemical name

### "Artificial Sweeteners Are Dangerous"
- FDA maximum safe sucralose: ~56 teaspoons/day
- No negative effects observed at 100x that level
- One of most thoroughly tested food additives
- Hundreds of studies; no confirmed harm at normal doses

---

## HOW TO EVALUATE CLAIMS

1. Does it violate calorie/macro fundamentals?
2. What does peer-reviewed research say?
3. Is the source selling something?
4. Does mechanism match reality?
5. Would this still work if calories were controlled?
