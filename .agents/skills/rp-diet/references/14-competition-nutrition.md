# Ch 14: Competition Day Nutrition
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## ENDURANCE SPORTS

### Week Before
- Adequate calories (not hypocaloric)
- Shift calories: less fat, more carbs
- Events >3 hrs: carb load 2-3 days out (+2-3 g/lb/day above normal)
- Moderate-high GI foods; avoid excess fiber

### Day Before
- Events >3 hrs: 3-5 g/lb above normal
- Events <1 hr: +0.5 g/lb above normal
- Spread evenly; finish by evening

### Pre-Race Meal (90-120 min before)
- Events >2 hrs: 0.5-1 g/lb carbs (max you can tolerate)
- Final sip of carbs within 15 min of start

### Intra-Race Carbs
| Duration | Min | Recommended | Max |
|----------|-----|-------------|-----|
| <1 hr | 0 | 0 | 30 g/hr |
| 1-2 hr | 30 g/hr | 45 g/hr | 60 g/hr |
| 2-3 hr | 45 g/hr | 60 g/hr | 90 g/hr |
| >3 hr | 60 g/hr | 90 g/hr | 90 g/hr |

- Max absorption: ~90 g/hr
- Increase fructose:glucose ratio (up to 1:2) for better absorption
- Practice during training

### Hydration
- Up to 1 L/hr without GI distress
- 6-8% sugar solution
- Drink every 10-15 min to maintain stomach volume
- Stay ahead of dehydration

## STRENGTH/POWER/COMBAT SPORTS

### Pre-Competition (3-4 days out)
- Full glycogen stores
- Adequate calories
- Familiar foods

### Multi-Event Day (hours between events)
- Low-fat, low-fiber foods
- Eat/drink immediately after each event
- Protein shakes + high-GI carbs

### Tournament Style (short breaks)
- Protein shakes + electrolyte carb drinks only
- Avoid solid food between matches

## PHYSIQUE COMPETITION PEAKING

### Timeline
Start 1.5 weeks before show. Must be at target leanness by then.

### Macros
- Protein: 1-1.5 g/lb/day
- Fat: ≥0.3 g/lb/day (helps hormone recovery)
- Carbs: manipulated for muscle fullness

### Carb Strategies

**1. Coasting In** (lowest risk)
- Adjust carbs based on appearance
- Flat muscles → increase carbs
- Holding water → decrease carbs

**2. Slow Ramp** (moderate risk)
- Add 50-100g carbs/day
- Target: ~5 g/lb by 72 hrs before show
- If bloating: cut carbs 40%

**3. Depletion/Supercompensation** (highest risk, highest reward)
- High-volume training + no carbs for 2-4 days
- 72 hrs out: high carbs (3-5 g/lb/day)
- Requires experience or expert coach

### Water
- 1.5 weeks out: +50% water intake (initiates diuresis)
- 1-2 days out: return to normal
- Mild reduction continues diuresis without antidiuretic response

### Salt
- Avoid manipulation unless experienced
- Risk outweighs reward for most

## WATER CUTS (Weight Class Sports)

**Max safe cut: 5% bodyweight**

| Time to Compete | Max Cut | Prep Time |
|-----------------|---------|-----------|
| 24 hrs | 5% | ~7 days |
| 12 hrs | 3% | ~4 days |
| 2 hrs | 2% | ~1 day |
| Mat-side | 1% | <1 day |

### Mat-Side Weigh-In
- Minimize water 12 hrs before
- Reduce food volume and salt 70%
- Endurance sports: no dehydration (just avoid bloat)

### 2-Hour Weigh-In
Starting day before:
- Low-volume foods
- Carbs ≤0.3 g/lb
- Salt -70%
- Stop water ~12 hrs before

Post weigh-in:
- Electrolyte drinks immediately
- High-GI carbs
- Gradual rehydration

### Rehydration
- Critical for performance
- Restore electrolytes + glycogen + fluids
- Consult physician before attempting
- Never exceed 5% cut; deaths have occurred
