# Ch 13: Special Diet Considerations
Book: The Renaissance Diet 2.0 | Authors: Dr. Mike Israetel, Melissa Davis, Jen Case, James Hoffmann

## TRAINING TWICE A DAY

### Principles
- Harder session first (safety + quality)
- At least 1 meal between sessions
- 5-6 meals/day recommended
- Sessions as far apart as possible

### Carb Classification for 2x/Day
| Workout 1 | Workout 2 | Classify As |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Light | Light | Moderate |
| Light | Moderate | Moderate |
| Light | Hard | Hard |
| Moderate | Moderate | Hard |
| Moderate | Hard | Hard |
| Hard | Hard | Hard × 1.15 |

### Fueling Strategy
- Intra-workout shake: fast carbs + whey during both sessions
- Between sessions: high-GI carbs, low fat/fiber
- Hydration: ~3g water per gram glycogen stored

## SPECIAL TIMING

### Early Morning Training (<1 hr after waking)
- Replace pre-workout meal with intra-workout shake
- Start sipping on wake, finish during workout
- Move fats to later meals

### Late Night Training (before bed)
- Combine post-workout + bedtime meal
- Increase shake carbs/protein to reduce solid food needed
- Move fats to earlier meals
- Skip veggies in post-workout if too full

### Skipped Meal
- Spread missed macros over next meals
- Fat loss: eat missed protein only (skip carbs/fat if not hungry)

## VEGAN DIETS

### Protein Adjustments
- Increase protein 20% above normal recommendations
- Most plant protein: incomplete amino acids or poor absorption
- Exceptions: soy, mycoprotein, nutritional yeast
- Combine sources for complete amino profile
- Competitive athletes: complete protein each meal

### Carbs
- No different from standard recommendations
- Note: many vegan proteins have ancillary carbs

### Fats
- Easier to eat healthy fats (plant sources)
- Avoid: coconut oil (saturated fat like butter)
- Best: avocado, vegetable oils, nuts

### Supplements for Vegans
- **B12**: Mandatory (not in plant foods)
- **Iron**: Often needed (non-heme less absorbed)
- **Creatine**: Vegans benefit more (no dietary creatine)
- **Omega-3**: Consider algae-based DHA/EPA

---

## PLANT-BASED CHALLENGES

### Mixed Macro Sources
- Many vegan proteins have carbs + fats
- Makes macro tracking harder
- Fat loss: choose leaner options, separate fat sources
- Muscle gain: mixed sources help hit calories easier

### Digestibility
- Plant cellulose reduces absorption
- Processed plant protein often better absorbed
- Higher protein targets compensate for lower absorption
